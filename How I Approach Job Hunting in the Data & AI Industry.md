# How I Approach Job Hunting in the Data & AI Industry

I believe many people have experienced nights during job hunting when anxiety makes it hard to fall asleep. This article was written during those moments. The reason of writing this article was to organize my thoughts more clearly and ease my anxiety. The ideas in this article are inspired by advice from senior students, videos I watched online, and conversations I had with people working in the industry.

## Before We Start

Again this article provided me with methodologies and ways of thinking during my job search when I felt lost. I know the job market is not great right now, so I always remind myself that job hunting is like a fraction. The numerator is always 1. But the denominator can be enormous, sometimes we have to try hundreds or even thousands of applications. 

**Expected Job = 1 / Unlimited Applications**

So I believe the first thing in job hunting is confidence. We have to believe that we will eventually get a job, just like the fraction above. 

From my perspective, the moat that once protected programmers who only knew how to write code is slowly disappearing. In the era of vibe coding, AI agents, and powerful tools, simply hand-writing code is becoming less valuable. What matters more now is understanding the logic behind the code, understanding the business problem, and being able to test and improve solutions. 

As a recent graduate majoring in data science, these are the skills I believe I need to focus on learning.

**Business Understanding + Data Skills + The Ability to Use AI Tools**. 

Okay, let's move on to the first step! 


## 1. Identifying High-Growth Industries through Government Policy
Government strategic plans often show which industries a country plans to support in the coming years, such as technological innovation, industrial upgrading, and national security–related sectors. These policies often guide where capital, research funding, and talent will go. Because of this, they can be a useful reference when identifying industries with long-term growth potential.

By reviewing government policy documents, technology roadmaps, and national strategies, we can identify industries that are likely to grow in the long run. In addition to widely discussed sectors such as **AI**, **Robotics**, and **Big Data**, there are also other industries that receive strong policy support but attract less public attention. These sectors may also offer significant long-term growth opportunities.They are:
- **Semiconductors & Advanced Computing**
- **Quantum Technology**
- **Cybersecurity and Digital Infrastructure**
- **Critical Minerals Resources**
- **Clean Energy**
- **Energy Infrastructure & Power Grid Modernization**
- **Biotechnology & Life Sciences**
- **Defense Technology**
- **Space Technology & the Commercial Space Economy**

Therefore, when selecting industries for further analysis or career exploration, it is reasonable to prioritize sectors that receive strong policy backing.


## 2. Selecting an Industry Before Choosing a Role  
When planning a career path, it is important to first identify industries with strong long-term growth potential, and then choose roles that match one’s skills and interests as much as possible. This helps align personal development with broader economic and technological trends. If there is no perfect match at the beginning, it may still be worthwhile to enter the industry first for better long-term opportunities. People are often shaped by their environment, an old example is: even a cleaner working on Wall Street might understand stocks better than many ordinary people. 


## 3. Selecting Companies Within an Industry

When choosing companies within an industry, I began to be just like the other students in CS, IT, DS or other tech fields aim to enter big tech companies. However, competition for these positions is extremely intense. Obviously that path does not work out, it can be worth considering startups that have recently raised Series A or Series B funding. A well-known example is Joseph Tsai, who left a high-paying job on Wall Street to join Alibaba in its early days.

There are several reasons why Series A or Series B startups can be attractive:

- **Team expansion after funding**  
  Companies that have just raised funding usually need to expand their teams quickly to grow the business.

- **Capital has already done part of the screening**  
  Venture capital firms conduct extensive due diligence before investing, so funded companies have already passed an initial level of evaluation.

- **Faster learning and real industry experience**  
  Startups often provide intense, hands-on experience in a rapidly growing industry. Even if the company fails or layoffs occur, the experience gained can still help you find new opportunities.

## 4. Which companies in Series A/B rounds are worth joining?

After identifying promising industries, the next step is to research startups that have recently raised **Series A or Series B funding**. At this stage, companies usually have validated products and early market traction, but still have significant growth potential.

In order to better evaluate these companies, it is helpful to conduct a structured industry and company analysis:

- **Industry environment analysis**  
  Use frameworks such as **SWOT analysis**, **Porter’s Five Forces**, or **PEST analysis** to understand the competitive environment and external factors affecting the industry.

- **Market size and adoption**  
  Examine indicators such as **market size, market share, and penetration rate** to understand the scale and growth potential of the industry.

- **Industry value chain**  
  Analyze the **upstream and downstream structure** of the industry to understand where companies create value and how different players interact.

- **Competitor analysis**  
  Identify the **top companies in the sector (for example, the top 5)** and analyze their current situation, founding team, strengths, and weaknesses.

- **Company positioning and future trends**  
  Evaluate the development stage of the industry, compare the advantages and disadvantages of different companies, and identify potential opportunities and risks for the company you want to join.


## 5. Aligning the Target Company and Role with Personal Experience

After researching the industry and identifying promising companies, the next step is to focus on the specific company and role you want to apply for. Carefully review the job description and understand the responsibilities required for the position.

Then connect your past experiences, projects, and skills with the requirements of the role. Highlight how your background matches the company’s needs and how your experience can contribute to the team.

## 6. Emphasize growth plan in your cover letter. 
In the cover letter, you can write a clear plan for professional growth in the role. This includes setting goals for the first few months, understanding daily responsibilities, and continuously building industry-specific knowledge and etc. 

A simple development roadmap might include:

- **First month**  
  Become familiar with the company’s products, tools, workflow, and team structure. Understand the main responsibilities of the role and be able to complete basic tasks independently.

- **Second to third month**  
  Gradually improve efficiency, take on more complex tasks, and begin contributing meaningful work to the team or project.

- **After one year**  
  Develop a deeper understanding of the industry and the company’s business model, and become someone who can solve problems independently and create value for the team.

- **Daily work and skill accumulation**  
  During daily work, continuously learn the professional knowledge required in the industry, including tools, technologies, business processes, and market dynamics.
